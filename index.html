Margot’s Mother

By Sofia Gukelberger

  • “It was my time to go,”
  • Margot’s mother would have said,
  • Had she still been alive.
  • Margot would have nodded
  • Clamping shut her lips, and
  • Changing the subject.
  • (This is how she always spoke to her mother
  • In the months before she died.)
  • Margot would have shoved that disagreement
  • Down as deep as it would go.
  • Now, after the funeral,
  • Margot wondered whether
  • She had done the right thing.
  • If she had disagreed, then maybe,
  • Would her mother have changed
  • Her mind?
  • Or did those tendrils of rage, of hate,
  • Of knowing that she knew best,
  • Of distrusting reason itself,
  • Take root in her mind long before
  • Margot even thought to disagree?
  • Did they make money when they
  • Told Margot’s mother
  • That the cure would kill her?
  • How much?
